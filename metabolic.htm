HGH Background

There are some anatomical, functional and hormonal changes that appear with aging among them a decrease in the activity of the Hypothalamus Axis - Hypophysis - Liver (GHRH - GH - IGF-I), which contributes to a decrease in the liver's production of Somatomedin-C; therefore, an increase in excess fat and a decrease in lean body mass. It is possible to revert the decrease in activity of this axis with a homeopathic formulation of growth hormone.

HGH Methods

21 adult patients between 45 and 86 years of age were divided into three groups and studied. Initially, all patients" plasmatic levels of Somatomedin-C were measured, their excess body fat calculated, blood chemistry analyzed, lipids profiled, functional hepatic tests administered and their immunoglobulin levels measured. Group 1 was treated for 5 months with 15 drops, 3 times a day orally with 21st Century HGH (homeopathic formulation of growth hormone); Group 2 also received 21st Century HGH in the same way. In addition, 15 drops 3 times a day orally of HE (homeopathic formulation of dried beef liver) were added during the 5 months of treatment; Group 3, the control group, was treated with a placebo.

Materials and methods of HGH

21 healthy adult subjects of both sexes between the ages of 45 and 86 years were studied, who were randomly assigned to one of three different groups. At the beginning of the study, a clinical history of all patients was performed and blood samples were taken for blood chemistry studies which included: blood glucose, urea, creatinine and uric acid; lipids profile: total cholesterol, cholesterol HDL, cholesterol LDL, triglycerides and atherogenic index; hepatic function tests: glutamic oxaloacetic transaminase, glutamic pyruvic transaminase, alkaline phosphatase, lactic dehydrogenase, bilirubin; immunoglobulins: IgG, IgA and IgM. Also determined were plasma levels of iron, calcium, amylase and somatomedin-C, HGH and excess body fat was calculated.

Somatometry data and the above-mentioned laboratory exams were repeated 5 months after the study was concluded.
